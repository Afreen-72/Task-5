Mastering the Smokey Eye

The smokey eye is a classic, sultry look that can be adapted for any occasion. Hereâ€™s a step-by-step guide to achieving it:

  1. Prime Your Lids

    Apply an eyeshadow primer to your eyelids. This will help your eyeshadow last longer, prevent creasing, and make the colors appear more vibrant.

  2. Lay Down Your Base Color

    Apply a neutral, matte eyeshadow shade (like a soft brown or grey) all over your eyelid, from lash line to brow bone. This will serve as a transition color.

  3. Define the Crease

    Using a fluffy blending brush, apply a darker matte shade (like a medium brown, charcoal, or deep plum) into your crease. Blend it outwards and upwards to create a soft, diffused effect. This is key for the "smokey" look.

  4. Apply the Darkest Shade

    Pat a dark, shimmery or matte eyeshadow (black, dark grey, deep navy, or dark brown) onto your eyelid, concentrating it closest to your lash line. Use a flat brush for this to get good color payoff. Blend the edges of this shade into the crease color.

  5. Smoke Out the Lower Lash Line

    Using a small pencil brush, apply the same dark eyeshadow you used on your lid along your lower lash line. Blend it out softly to create a cohesive smokey effect. You can also add a touch of the crease color over this.

  6. Inner Corner and Brow Bone Highlight

    Apply a shimmery, light eyeshadow or highlighter to your inner corner and just under your brow bone. This will brighten your eyes and add dimension.

  7. Add Eyeliner and Mascara

    Line your upper and lower waterlines with black kohl eyeliner for intensity. Finish with several generous coats of volumizing mascara to complete the dramatic smokey eye.

With practice, you'll master this iconic look and feel incredibly glamorous!
